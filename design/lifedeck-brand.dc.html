Lifedeck
Brand identity · v1

Calm command over your whole life.

Lifedeck isn’t a to-do list — it’s a personal control panel for your days, lists, routines, goals and the people you share them with. The identity carries that: a quiet deck of cards, held in one hand, arranged at a glance. Built on the existing system — indigo/violet OKLCH, system type, soft geometry.

01 — Brandmark concepts
CHOSEN
A. The Deck

Three cards, cleanly stacked and held — your life as an ordered deck at a glance. The violet dot is the live “now”: calm, in control, never busy.

B. The Console

A cockpit bento — each panel an area of life under one calm surface. Strong as an app tile, but reads more “dashboard SaaS” than personal, and leans away from the deck.

C. The Hand

A fanned hand of cards — warm and human for a couple. Lively, but the rotation feels playful rather than “calm command,” and blurs sooner at 16px.

02 — Finalized mark · “The Deck”
Construction
32-unit grid · cards 15×20, radius 4.5 · 2.5u offset stagger · dot Ø4.2 at the front card’s upper third.

Two background cards differentiate by tint (full-colour) or opacity 0.32 / 0.58 (mono). Few elements, generous overlap — the silhouette stays a deck even when small.

07 — Surfaces & usage

Mono · currentColor + knockout dot
Do
  • Keep the card stagger and overlap fixed — scale as one unit.
  • Use violet only for the single live dot.
  • Give it clear space of one front-card width.
  • Switch to the mono knockout on busy or coloured surfaces.
✕Don’t
  • Don’t rotate, fan, or re-space the cards.
  • Don’t add gradients, shadows or 3-D to the mark.
  • Don’t recolour cards or use violet as a card fill.
  • Don’t stretch the wordmark or change its tracking.
